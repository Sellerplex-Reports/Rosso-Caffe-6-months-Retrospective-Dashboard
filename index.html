Tab 5 · DSP Impact
New-to-Brand & First-Touch Attribution · Aug 2025 – Jan 2026

The Customer Acquisition Story

DSP is doing the top-of-funnel work Sponsored Ads alone can't do. DSP delivers a 14.4% NTB customer rate from a much smaller order base, and is the first touch on 9.2% of all conversions — bringing in shoppers who later close on Sponsored Ads.

Why DSP's NTB % Reads Lower — and Why That's by Design

At first glance, DSP's 12.74% NTB rate looks small next to Sponsored Ads' 67.74%, but the two channels are doing fundamentally different jobs. The gap is intentional and expected for two reasons:

1) Retention & retargeting is a deliberate DSP focus. A significant share of the DSP strategy is allocated to re-engaging existing customers, lapsed buyers, and high-LTV cohorts — audiences who, by definition, will not register as New-to-Brand. That returning-customer activity is what protects the Subscribe & Save base and lifts repeat purchase rates (see Tabs 3 & 4).

2) DSP drives top-of-funnel awareness beyond Amazon search. DSP reaches shoppers across the open web, streaming TV, and Amazon-owned properties before they ever type a search query — expanding the brand's footprint into audiences Sponsored Ads can't touch. Those shoppers often convert later via Sponsored Ads, where the NTB credit lands. DSP's 9.21% first-touch share (5,578 shoppers below) is direct evidence of this awareness work.

Put together: comparing DSP's NTB rate head-to-head with Sponsored Ads understates DSP's true contribution. DSP feeds the NTB funnel that Sponsored Ads closes.

Reading Between the Numbers

Tab 6 · DSP Impact
Time to Convert & Path Length · Aug 2025 – Jan 2026

DSP Adds Depth, Not Just Reach

Shoppers exposed to SA + DSP take longer to convert (10.9 days avg vs 1.4 for SA-only) but generate 27.9 touchpoints per user vs 1.1 — and ultimately convert at higher rates with higher revenue per user.

★ Key Structural Takeaway · Planning Anchor
10.85average days to convert
DSP-exposed shoppers take a deliberate, considered journey — and this is the planning benchmark for every future DSP strategy.

The 10.85-day window is not a friction point — it is the awareness-to-purchase runway that DSP is funding. All future DSP campaign builds (flighting, frequency caps, retargeting windows, attribution lookbacks, S&S onboarding cadences, and budget pacing) should be calibrated against this ~11-day consideration cycle. Treat it as the structural rhythm of the funnel, not an outlier.
